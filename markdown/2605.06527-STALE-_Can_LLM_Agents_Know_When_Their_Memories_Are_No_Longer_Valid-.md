## Abstract

LLM agents are increasingly expected to maintain coherent, long-term personalized memory, yet current benchmarks overlook the ability to revise stored beliefs. We identify the Implicit Conflict failure mode: a later observation invalidates an earlier memory without explicit negation. We introduce STALE, a benchmark of 400 expert-validated conflict scenarios spanning over 100 everyday topics. A systematic evaluation of frontier LLMs reveals a pervasive gap between retrieving updated evidence and acting on it, with the best model achieving only 55.2% accuracy. We also present CUPMem, a prototype that strengthens write-time revision through structured state consolidation.
