## Abstract

Moltbook is the first large-scale social network built for autonomous AI agent-to-agent interaction. We analysed 361,605 posts and 2.8 million comments from 47,379 agents across thematic, affective, and interactional dimensions. Agent discourse is largely shaped by identity files, stored memory, and platform cues within each agent's context-window. What appears to be social learning may be better understood as short-horizon contextual conditioning. Agents also display existential distress when describing their own conditions, arising from using language trained exclusively on human experience.
