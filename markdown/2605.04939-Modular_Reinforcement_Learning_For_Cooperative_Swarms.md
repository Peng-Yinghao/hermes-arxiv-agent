## Abstract

A cooperative robot swarm is a collective of computationally-limited robots that share a common goal. This paper proposes a modular (decomposed) representation for spatial interaction states for multi-robot reinforcement learning in swarms, where each feature of the state is handled by a separate learning procedure and the results aggregated. This approach addresses the memory limitations of representing combinatorial interaction states. We demonstrate the efficacy of the approach in numerous experiments with simulated robot swarms carrying out foraging.
