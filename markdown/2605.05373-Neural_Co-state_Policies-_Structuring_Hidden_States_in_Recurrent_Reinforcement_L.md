## Abstract

This paper establishes a formal link between hidden states in recurrent policies and the Pontryagin minimum principle (PMP) from optimal control. We demonstrate that for standard recurrent architectures, latent representations map directly to PMP co-states, allowing the readout layer to be interpreted as performing Hamiltonian minimization. We introduce a PMP-derived co-state loss to explicitly structure internal dynamics. Empirically, this approach matches or improves performance on partially observable DMControl tasks, and is robust against zero-shot out-of-distribution sensor masking.
