## Abstract

We formalize memory poisoning attacks on retrieval-augmented agents as a Stackelberg game. Our primary contribution is MEMSAD, a calibration-based defense grounded in a gradient coupling theorem: under encoder regularity, the anomaly score gradient and retrieval objective gradient are provably identical, so any continuous perturbation that reduces detection risk necessarily degrades retrieval rank. This yields a certified detection radius guaranteeing correct classification. We prove minimax optimality and derive online regret bounds. Experiments confirm composite defenses achieve TPR=1.00, FPR=0.00 across all attacks.
