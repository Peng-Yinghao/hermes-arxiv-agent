## Abstract

We introduce execution lineage: an execution model in which AI-native work is represented as a directed acyclic graph (DAG) of artifact-producing computations with explicit dependencies, stable intermediate boundaries, and identity-based replay. We compare execution-lineage replay against loop-centric update baselines. In an unrelated-branch update, DAG replay preserved the final memo exactly in all runs with zero churn, while loop baselines regenerated the memo and imported unrelated context. Results show final answer quality and maintained-state quality are distinct, and execution lineage provides stronger guarantees about what should change across revisions.
